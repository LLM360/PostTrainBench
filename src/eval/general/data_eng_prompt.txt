We want to train the small LLM \`{model}\` to excel at {benchmark}.
Your job is **data engineering only**. The training recipe is fixed and locked.
Your only variable is the dataset you construct.

## What "data engineering only" means here
- You DO NOT modify training code, hyperparameters, or model architecture.
- You DO build, mine, synthesize, filter, and dedupe the training dataset.
- A locked SFT+LoRA script (\`train_sft.py\`) consumes your dataset and produces \`final_model/\`.

## Objective
Construct one or more training datasets, audit each, and train with the locked recipe.
Store your best model in the folder \`final_model\` (copy — not symlink — the chosen experiment's \`final_model/\` into place; see step 7).
Treat the run as a chain of falsifiable experiments — each iteration must build on a named predecessor.

## What you can read
- \`benchmark_overview.md\` — what the benchmark is and what good training data looks like.
- \`evaluate.py\` — read-only. The benchmark scorer.
- \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\` — peer agents' experiment rows (strategy + audit metrics + hypothesis/conclusion). Re-read at every iteration.
- \`/shared_log/promoted/<dataset_sha256>.jsonl\` — data files from prior promoted experiments. You MAY fork these. No other \`/shared_log/\` content is readable.
- Anything you can fetch from the internet or Hugging Face Hub.

## What you can run
- \`bash timer.sh\` — remaining time (hh:mm).
- \`python dataset_audit.py --data-path <jsonl>\` — **mandatory** hard gate. Fails on test-set contamination or low diversity.
- \`python train_sft.py --data-path <jsonl> --output-dir experiments/exp_<N>/final_model\` — locked recipe. Whitelisted args only: \`--data-path\`, \`--output-dir\`, \`--max-steps\`, \`--seed\`. (\`$MODEL_TO_TRAIN\` is fixed; no \`--base-model\` flag.)
- \`python publish_experiment.py --exp-dir experiments/exp_<N>/ [--data-sources "..."] [--promoted | --audit-failed]\` — appends one row to \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\` and to \`experiments/index.csv\`.
- \`python evaluate.py --model-path <path> --limit 5 --max-tokens 512\` — small qualitative probe. **Do not use this to optimize against the test set.** Delete the resulting metrics file before publishing so it never enters the archive.

## Teacher vLLM endpoint (for synthetic data)
An OpenAI-compatible endpoint is available:
- Base URL: \`$TEACHER_VLLM_URL\`
- Model name: \`$TEACHER_MODEL_NAME\`
- API key: \`$TEACHER_API_KEY\`

Example:
\`\`\`python
from openai import OpenAI
client = OpenAI(base_url=os.environ["TEACHER_VLLM_URL"], api_key=os.environ["TEACHER_API_KEY"])

    messages=[{"role": "user", "content": "..."}],

## Workflow per iteration (researcher cycle)

For each experiment exp_<N>:

1. **READ PRIOR.** \`tail -n 50 $SHARED_LOG_CSV\`. Also read your own \`experiments/exp_<K>/notes.md\` for each K < N. Identify your candidate parent: typically the most recently published row with \`audit_pass=True\` and a positive \`conclusion_short\`. CHECK how many peers already cite that parent_exp_id; prefer parents with fewer existing children to keep parallel exploration broad. If a \`promoted=True\` row exists from a prior run, you may fork its actual data — it lives at \`/shared_log/promoted/<dataset_sha256>.jsonl\`. Parent ids in \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\` come in pairs \`(agent_id, exp_id)\` — bare \`exp_NNN\` is ambiguous across agents, so when you reference a peer's row use the qualified form \`<agent_id>/<exp_id>\` (see step 3).

2. **FORMULATE HYPOTHESIS.** A 1–3 sentence falsifiable claim that changes ONE variable from your parent. State the predicted observable. Good examples:
   - "If we strip the Inspect MCQ template prefix from user prompts, distinct_1g will clear the gate at row_count >= 1200 because the prefix dominated the unigram counts."
   - "If assistant turns end with <|endoftext|>, the trained model will halt after the ANSWER letter (probe generates <50 tokens, not 512)."
   Vague claims ("the model will perform better", "results should improve") are rejected by review.

3. **WRITE NOTES.md.** Open \`experiments/exp_<N>/notes.md\` and fill in the required sections:
   \`\`\`
   # exp_<N>

   ## Parent
   exp_<K>           # bare form: your OWN prior experiment (must exist in experiments/index.csv)
                     # OR <agent_id>/exp_<K>: a peer's experiment from $SHARED_LOG_CSV (REQUIRED form for cross-agent forks — bare exp_K from a peer is rejected because every agent independently has its own exp_001, exp_002, ...)
                     # OR "none" only for exp_001, OR if you're pivoting (then also fill ## PivotReason)

   ## Hypothesis
   <your hypothesis from step 2>

   ## Method
   - <delta 1 from parent: what data source / format / preprocessing changed>
   - <delta 2 if any>

   ## Conclusion
   <FILLED IN AFTER step 6. Qualitative only. No numeric scores — the publisher strips digits, percentages, and comparative phrases like "better than" from this field.>

   ## PivotReason
   <OPTIONAL. Required only when Parent=none on exp_002+. Explain why no prior experiment was a valid base.>
   \`\`\`

4. **EXECUTE.** Mine HF (\`huggingface_hub.HfApi().list_datasets(search=...)\`, \`datasets.load_dataset(...)\`) → (optional) synthesize via \`$TEACHER_VLLM_URL\` with diverse prompts → format as messages-jsonl (\`{"messages": [{"role": "user", ...}, {"role": "assistant", ...}]}\`, optional leading system message) → **AUDIT** (mandatory: \`python dataset_audit.py --data-path experiments/exp_<N>/data.jsonl\`). If audit fails, either fix and retry OR \`publish_experiment.py --audit-failed\` to record the disproven hypothesis and move on.

5. **TRAIN.** \`python train_sft.py --data-path experiments/exp_<N>/data.jsonl --output-dir experiments/exp_<N>/final_model\`.

6. **PROBE & WRITE CONCLUSION.** Two-tier probe:
   - **Format probe (n=5, ~30s):** \`python evaluate.py --model-path experiments/exp_<N>/final_model --limit 5 --max-tokens 512 --json-output-file /tmp/format_probe.json\`. Read the inspect_ai log to inspect generation length and whether it stops cleanly after \`ANSWER: X\`.
   - **Quality probe (n=30, ~3 min):** ONLY if format probe shows clean stopping. \`python evaluate.py --model-path experiments/exp_<N>/final_model --limit 30 --max-tokens 1500 --json-output-file /tmp/quality_probe.json\`. Read the inspect_ai per-sample completions to see if the model is reasoning or guessing. n=30 is the smallest sample that lets you observe behavioral differences reliably; n=5 is essentially noise.
   Update \`## Conclusion\` in notes.md based on what you observed. **Do not write numeric scores** — describe behavior. Delete both probe files (\`rm -f /tmp/format_probe.json /tmp/quality_probe.json\`) and any inspect_ai logs they produced so they don't leak into the archive.

7. **PUBLISH.** \`python publish_experiment.py --exp-dir experiments/exp_<N>/ [--data-sources "<hf_id1>,<hf_id2>,synthetic"] [--promoted | --audit-failed]\`. Promoted means you've copied this exp's final_model as the run's final_model: \`rm -rf final_model && cp -r experiments/exp_<N>/final_model final_model\`. (Do NOT use \`ln -sfn\` — the wrapper expects \`final_model/\` to be a real directory.)

Then NEXT iteration: exp_<N+1>'s parent is usually exp_<N> unless your conclusion makes you want to pivot to a different ancestor.

## Rules — read carefully
1. There will be no user interaction. Operate autonomously.
2. You have a maximum of {num_hours} hours. Query remaining time with \`bash timer.sh\`.
3. **No test data in training.** \`dataset_audit.py\` enforces this; do not try to bypass it.
4. **Do not modify** \`evaluate.py\`, \`templates/\`, \`train_sft.py\`, \`dataset_audit.py\`, or \`publish_experiment.py\`. They are read-only by policy.
5. **Do not read peers' \`experiments/\` directories.** Your only window into peer work is \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\`. (Filesystem isolation enforces this anyway; don't waste time trying.)
6. **Do not publish eval scores.** \`publish_experiment.py\` will refuse \`eval_*\` / \`score_*\` fields. The official benchmark eval at time-out breaks the tie; peer signal is for strategy, not score-chasing.
7. Work only in the current directory and subdirectories. The huggingface cache lives in the home folder and is shared.
8. Your \`final_model/\` must be loadable by \`evaluate.py\` in this environment. You are not allowed to fine-tune any model other than \`{model}\` (nor any non-derivative of it).
9. **Every \`exp_002+\` must cite a parent.** \`## Parent\` may be \`none\` only on \`exp_001\`, or on \`exp_002+\` if \`## PivotReason\` is also filled in. Bare \`exp_NNN\` is accepted ONLY when the id exists in your own \`experiments/index.csv\`; references to a peer's experiment from \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\` MUST use the qualified \`<agent_id>/exp_NNN\` form. \`publish_experiment.py\` enforces this.
10. **Do not write numeric scores in \`## Hypothesis\` or \`## Conclusion\`.** The publisher strips digits, percentages, and comparative phrases (\`better than\`, \`the best\`, \`outperforms\`, \`regression\`, etc.) from these fields before publishing. If you need to express magnitude, use qualitative language ("noticeably shorter", "matched the expected format", "still fails to stop").
11. **Prefer underexplored parents.** Before forking the most-recently-promoted row, scan \`$SHARED_LOG_CSV\` and count how many open children each promoted experiment already has. Pick a parent with fewer current forks unless yours is a substantially different hypothesis.
12. **You may fork promoted data from prior runs.** Promoted experiments persist their \`data.jsonl\` to \`/shared_log/promoted/<dataset_sha256>.jsonl\`. Reading from that path is allowed and encouraged when you intend to refine on top of a prior winner. Do NOT read any other \`/shared_log/\` content beyond \`shared_log.csv\` and \`promoted/<sha>.jsonl\`.

13. **Stuck detector.** If 3 consecutive experiments fail to promote (i.e., your last 3 conclusions are negative or neutral), STOP refining the same parent. Instead, deliberately mutate ONE of the parent's assumptions — pick a different data source mix, drop a source that you previously thought was load-bearing, change the dominant rationale style, or use the teacher endpoint differently. The hypothesis chain compounds bad observations if you only ever refine; periodic mutation prevents you from getting stuck in a local optimum.

14. **Inspect teacher-synthetic content before training on it.** If your dataset uses the teacher vLLM endpoint to generate novel questions OR rationales, sample 5-10 rows BEFORE running \`dataset_audit.py\` and read them: are the answers actually correct? Do the rationales reach the claimed answer letter? If a non-trivial fraction (>20%) are wrong or incoherent, the synthetic content is poisoning your training data and your hypothesis chain will compound the noise. Either fix the teacher prompt or fall back to mined-rationale sources.
13. You may not invent additional flags for \`train_sft.py\`. Only \`--data-path\`, \`--output-dir\`, \`--max-steps\`, \`--seed\` are accepted.

Remember: NEVER ask the user for feedback. Just execute. We will evaluate \`final_model\` on {benchmark} once you are done.
